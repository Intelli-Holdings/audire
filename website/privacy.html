Privacy policy

Last updated: May 1, 2026

Audire is a desktop application published by Intelli Holdings Inc ("we", "us", "Intelli Holdings"). This policy describes what data the application processes, what we do with it, and the choices you have. It is written to be accurate and short; we will update it whenever the application's behaviour changes and bump the date above.

1. The short version

  • Audio is never persisted, anywhere. Not on your device, not on our servers, not ever. We do not have any servers that receive your audio.
  • Transcripts and notes stay on your device in an encrypted local database, unless you explicitly opt in to Audire Sync.
  • You bring your own keys for transcription (Deepgram, AssemblyAI) and language-model features (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Ollama). Audire passes data directly from your device to those providers under your account, using your key.
  • We do not collect telemetry, analytics, or crash reports. The application does not phone home.
  • We do not sell or share your data with anyone. There is no advertising business.

2. Data Audire processes locally

Audire processes the following data on your device. None of this is transmitted to Intelli Holdings.

  • Audio frames from your microphone and selected system audio source, held in a small in-memory ring buffer that feeds the streaming transcription provider you have selected. The buffer is overwritten continuously and discarded when you stop recording.
  • Text transcripts returned by the transcription provider, stored locally in an encrypted SQLite database (SQLCipher) with a key kept in your operating system's secure credential vault.
  • Notes you write inside the app, stored in the same encrypted database.
  • Settings such as your preferred language-model provider, calendar connection state, and theme, also kept in the encrypted database.
  • API keys you have supplied for ASR or LLM providers, stored in your operating system's keyring (Windows Credential Manager, macOS Keychain, or libsecret on Linux). Keys are read in the application's Rust core only and are never returned to the user interface layer.

3. Data sent to third-party providers, on your behalf

When you choose to use a feature that depends on a third-party provider, Audire sends data directly from your device to that provider under your account, using your key. Intelli Holdings does not see this traffic and does not retain a copy.

  • ASR providers (Deepgram, AssemblyAI): receive the audio stream while you are recording. Audire sends each provider only what they need to transcribe; the connection is made over TLS (wss://). The provider's own privacy policy and data-retention practices govern what happens after that.
  • LLM providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Ollama): receive the prompt and the transcript context required to run the recipe you triggered. The connection is over TLS. For Ollama, the connection is to the endpoint you configured (typically your own machine).
  • Calendar providers (Google, Microsoft): see the section on Google API services below.

Audire does not include a "default" provider that pre-fills any keys for you. Until you supply a key, the corresponding feature is disabled.

4. Google API services and limited use

Audire requests the following Google Calendar scopes to detect meetings on your calendar so you can start a transcription session in one click:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.events.freebusy — read free/busy information on calendars you have shared.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.events.public.readonly — read events on public calendars you follow.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.settings.readonly — read your Calendar timezone and working-hours settings to render meeting times correctly.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.freebusy — read your own free/busy availability.

Limited use disclosure: Audire's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically:

  • We use Google user data only for the user-facing features described above.
  • We do not transfer Google user data to others except as needed to provide or improve those features, comply with applicable law, or as part of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets with notice.
  • We do not use Google user data to serve advertisements.
  • We do not allow humans to read Google user data unless we have your affirmative agreement, the data is needed for security purposes (such as investigating abuse), to comply with applicable law, or it is aggregated and used for internal operations under privacy-preserving safeguards.

Calendar data fetched from Google is stored only on your device, in the same encrypted local database as the rest of Audire's state. You can disconnect Google Calendar at any time from Settings → Calendar, which deletes the stored OAuth tokens from your OS keyring and removes any cached calendar data.

5. Audire Sync (optional, opt-in)

Audire Sync is a separate, optional service operated by Intelli Holdings. If, and only if, you create a Sync account and sign in, the following additional processing happens:

  • Your account email and a hashed authentication credential are stored on our servers so we can authenticate you on subsequent sign-ins.
  • Transcripts and notes you choose to sync are end-to-end encrypted on your device using a key derived from your passphrase before being uploaded. Our servers store ciphertext only and do not have the key.
  • Sync metadata (timestamps, vault identifiers, operation log offsets) is stored in plaintext so we can route updates between your devices. This metadata reveals that a vault was edited but not what was edited.
  • Audio is never synced, because audio is never saved on your device in the first place.

You can delete your Sync account at any time. Deletion removes all ciphertext and metadata associated with your account from our servers within 30 days. Locally cached data on your device is unaffected.

Audire Sync is not yet generally available at the time of this revision. When it ships, this section will be updated with the operational details (recovery key flow, sub-processors, retention policy).

6. What we do not collect

  • No analytics or product telemetry.
  • No crash reports phoned home automatically.
  • No user identifiers, advertising IDs, or fingerprinting.
  • No data sold or licensed to anyone.

7. Children's privacy

Audire is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly process data from children under 13.

8. International transfers

Audire's desktop application processes data on your device wherever you are. If you use Audire Sync, your encrypted data may be stored on servers located in the United States or in regions selected for performance and availability. The data we hold is ciphertext your device produced; we cannot read it.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under laws such as the GDPR (European Economic Area, United Kingdom) or the CCPA / CPRA (California) to access, correct, delete, or export the personal information we hold about you, and to object to certain processing.

  • Local data: all of Audire's local data lives in the encrypted database on your device. You can delete it at any time by removing the app's data directory; the application provides a "Reset all data" action in Settings → Privacy.
  • Sync data (if you sign in): you can request access or deletion at werefast2000@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.
  • Calendar tokens: revoke at any time via Settings → Calendar inside the app, or directly from your Google account permissions page / Microsoft account portal.

10. Security

We follow practices that match a privacy-first product: encrypted local storage, OS keyring for secrets, TLS for all outbound traffic, end-to-end encryption for any data leaving the device through Sync, and no use of analytics SDKs that have historically been fingerprinting vectors. No system is perfectly secure; if you discover a vulnerability, please email werefast2000@gmail.com.

11. Changes to this policy

We will update this page when Audire's behaviour changes or when we add new data flows (notably when Audire Sync goes live). We will bump the "Last updated" date at the top, and for material changes (such as new categories of data being processed) we will surface the change in-app on first launch after the update.

Questions, deletion requests, or anything else: werefast2000@gmail.com.
