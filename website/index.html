How Audire works

Quiet capture, clean notes, and explicit data boundaries from the first run.

01

Capture the audio you choose

Stream microphone and system audio into the transcription provider you configure. Audire does not write audio files to disk.

02

Turn transcripts into notes

Use recipes for summaries, decisions, action items, weekly recaps, interview notes, and follow-up drafts.

03

Keep the record local

Transcripts and notes live in SQLCipher at rest, with database keys stored in your operating system keyring.

Privacy, by construction.

Audire is designed around a simple constraint: there is nowhere in the app for stored audio to live. The transcript can become useful notes, but the raw audio stream is discarded.

Why Audire requests Google Calendar access

Audire integrates with Google Calendar to detect meetings you have scheduled and offer a one-click way to start a transcription session when one begins. The integration is read-only and uses the smallest set of scopes required to do this.

ScopeWhy Audire requests it
calendar.events.freebusy Read free/busy windows on calendars you have shared with the user, so Audire can show your upcoming meeting load on the home screen.
calendar.events.public.readonly Read events on public calendars the user follows, such as team broadcast calendars, to surface them as upcoming in the app.
calendar.settings.readonly Read your Calendar timezone and working-hours settings so Audire renders meeting times correctly on your device.
calendar.freebusy Read your own availability so Audire can detect a meeting that is starting and prompt to begin a transcription session.

Audire never writes to your calendar, never reads private event contents beyond what these scopes return, and never shares calendar data with any third party. Calendar data stays on your device. The full data flow is described in the privacy policy.
