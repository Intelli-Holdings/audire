How Audire works

Quiet capture, clean notes, and explicit data boundaries from the first run.

01

Capture the audio you choose

Stream microphone and system audio into the transcription provider you configure. Audire does not write audio files to disk.

02

Turn transcripts into notes

Use recipes for summaries, decisions, action items, weekly recaps, interview notes, and follow-up drafts.

03

Keep the record local

Transcripts and notes live in SQLCipher at rest, with database keys stored in your operating system keyring.

Works where your meetings already happen.

Audire captures from your computer instead of sending a bot into the call, so it can support meetings across conferencing tools, webinars, demos, and screen-share reviews.

Key takeaways

Next steps

Get notes in the format your team needs.

Turn the same transcript into a customer interview, action list, executive summary, or weekly recap. Recipes are reusable prompts that run with the model provider you choose.

Privacy, by construction.

Audire is designed around a simple constraint: there is nowhere in the app for stored audio to live. The transcript can become useful notes, but the raw audio stream is discarded.

Audio never persisted

No local audio column, no cloud upload, no sync path for recordings.

Your provider accounts

ASR and LLM calls use keys you supply and go directly from your device.

No telemetry

No analytics, no crash reporter, no first-run beacon.

Put your meetings to work.

Ask for a follow-up email, extract open questions, write a blog post, or summarize decisions. Audire keeps these workflows close to your own transcript and notes.
