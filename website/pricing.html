Pricing that respects your data and your wallet.

Audire is free to use locally, forever. The optional Sync add-on makes it easy to keep your notes encrypted and synced across devices — without ever giving up control of the underlying audio.

Bring your own keys for AI

Transcription (Deepgram, AssemblyAI) and language-model features (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Ollama) run on your own provider account using your own API key. Audire never charges you for AI usage and never proxies that traffic — audio and prompts go from your device straight to the provider you chose, over TLS.

That keeps the privacy story airtight (we don't see your data) and keeps the pricing honest (you pay providers what they charge, nothing more).

Frequently asked

Why is the local app free forever?

Audire is open source under the MIT license. Anyone can build the app from source themselves — charging for the binary would be silly. The optional Sync service is what we charge for, because running encrypted storage and a sync server costs real money.

Why do I have to bring my own keys?

ASR and LLM providers bill by the minute or by the token, and their pricing changes faster than ours could keep up with. By having you put a key directly into Audire, your audio and prompts never pass through Audire's infrastructure — we physically can't see them, and you pay providers exactly what they charge with no Audire markup.

What happens to my audio?

Nothing. Audire never persists audio — not on your device, not in our cloud, not ever. While you're recording, audio lives in a small in-memory ring buffer that feeds the transcription provider over a TLS WebSocket, and is overwritten continuously. The local database has no audio column, so there is nowhere to put it even if a future bug tried.

Will Sync ever include managed AI usage?

We've reserved the option to add a higher Sync tier that includes a monthly ASR/LLM credit pool, for people who don't want to manage provider accounts. It is explicitly out of scope for v1. If we ship it, the privacy guarantees would change (Audire would have provider-side usage logs) and we'd surface that clearly — the BYOK tiers would never go away.

Do you offer education or non-profit discounts?

Yes. Once Sync launches, students, faculty, and non-profit employees will be eligible for a discount — details on the pricing page at launch. Email us at the address below if you're interested in bulk licensing or have a question that doesn't fit one of these tiers.

Can I self-host the Sync server?

The Sync server is being designed to be self-hostable on Fly + Neon, but the open-source release of the server lags the hosted service. If self-hosting is a hard requirement for you, get in touch — we'd like to hear about your use case before we commit to a public release date.
